About Kampala University

Kampala University was established by corporate professionals in February 1998. It opened its gates to pioneer students in October 1999. The university was first licensed by the Ministry of Education and Sports on November 23, 2000, to operate as an institution of higher learning. In 2003, KU held its 1st graduation ceremony with a total of 141 graduates.

The institution is fully recognised and accredited by the National Council for Higher Education (NCHE). In 2014, the university applied for and was granted a charter on March 23, 2016, by the government of Uganda, duly signed by His Excellency, Gen. Yoweri Kaguta Museveni, the President of the Republic of Uganda. In 2018, Kampala University was gazetted, making its operations, curriculum, and credentials fully recognised nationally, regionally, and internationally.

Language of Instruction

The medium of instruction and assessment is English. KU has an English and Communication Skills Module for all students with challenges in English

Shaping Futures

We nurture talent, inspire excellence, and equip students with the knowledge to make a lasting impact in their communities and beyond.

Qualification Framework

KU follows the Uganda National Qualification Framework established by NCHE.

Excellence in Education

We are committed to providing high-quality, student-centered learning that fosters innovation, critical thinking, and excellence.

Sports

At KU, we honor athletic excellence, teamwork, and the proud spirit of our athletes, celebrating every achievement and challenge on the journey to success.

Academic Character

KU is a secular institution that emphasises quality, relevance, good moral character, entrepreneurship, flexibility, research, and trust in God. The University aims at producing all-round graduates who shall be vanguards of change in their respective communities
